The arc

Support taught me what dev has to fix.

I started in customer support a decade ago, deep in the inbox of growing DTC brands. I saw the same tickets again and again — slow product pages, broken checkouts, email flows that never sent, missing tracking. The patterns weren't customer problems. They were storefront problems.

So I learned to fix them. Liquid, CSS, JavaScript, Klaviyo, schema, 3PL workflows. The dev work wasn't a pivot away from support — it was the natural next step. Every storefront decision I make now is informed by the support ticket it would generate three weeks later.

That dual lens still shapes how I work: I build for the user on the page, the customer who'll email two weeks after purchase, and the operator who has to keep it running. Every store on this timeline is one I built end-to-end and supported through real launches, real returns, and real growth.
